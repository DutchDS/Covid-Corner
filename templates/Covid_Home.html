About the Risk Calculator

The Covid-19 virus for the last few months has caused many people around the world to change their normal habits. It is teaching us to be more observant on the activities we do and the people we meet with. This new habit can be a good thing, but can also cause anxiety for people. For those who are feeling ill, but do not know if they should go see a doctor may grow in anxiety. The fear of having Covid-19 can cause sleepless nights for some. The Risk Calculator was created to show information on what people may do in regards to symptoms, but as well show only data without the negative externalities of media. Disclaimer: This is not medical advice, and the models mentioned in the following are not 100% accurate for information on Covid-19 is still dynamic. The Risk Calculator uses various machine-learning models to help predict if someone may need to go the doctor to see if they have the virus. The models used for the Risk Calculator are the decision-tree model, the random forest model, and the K-nearest neighbors model. The models are not 100% accurate, and are still in beta due to the ever changing data incorporated with Covid-19. This is just here to show information.
